**Racing United** is a simulator and a celebration of automotive history, ingenuity, and culture. Drive, modify and tune any vehicle for any driving style and chosen sport. The game aspired to cover the full range of motorports and culture like the illegal street racing scene world wide. This game is open source and crowd sourced. Meaning its built in collaboration with the community. Tutorials and guides will be readily available and regularly updated, constantly refining our craft to develop content faster and better.
---

## Vision

Racing United is built around the idea that the automotive world is more than just racing.  
It is a deep, immersive world of:

- Cars
- Motorcycles
- Motorsport engineering
- Tuning culture
- Grand touring and scenic driving
- Car meets and community events
- Historical vehicle preservation
- Open-road and free-roam exploration

This project aims to capture that full spirit in an accessible and community-built simulator.

---

## Core Features (Planned)

- Open-source and community-driven development
- Free-roam environments inspired by real-world roads and landscapes
- Vehicle building and tuning systems
- Functional visual modifications
- In-depth text-based mechanical tuning
- Multiple motorsport disciplines
- Road cars, race cars, motorcycles, karts, trucks, and more
- Community-made assets, tools, and documentation
- Focus on automotive heritage, driving feel, and enthusiast culture

---

## Gameplay Philosophy

Racing United gives players both the **tools** and the **methodology** to shape vehicles around their chosen driving style and discipline.

Players will be able to:

- Build vehicles for different motorsport categories
- Customize visual appearance with meaningful functional context
- Tune setups through mechanical and text-based systems
- Explore both legal motorsport environments and underground / street racing themes
- Learn through experimentation, guides, and community knowledge

## Open Source & Community Contribution

This project is intended to be **open source** and **community-built**.

The long-term goal is to create a platform where:

- Artists can contribute 3D assets
- Programmers can help build systems and tools
- Writers can create documentation and tutorials
- Testers can help refine gameplay
- Automotive enthusiasts can share knowledge and historical references

Tutorials and guides will be made available and updated over time to help contributors create content faster and with better consistency.
